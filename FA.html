ğŸ“š 2. Accounting Concepts and Conventions

These are the fundamental rules that form the base of accounting.

ğŸ”¸ 2.1 Going Concern Assumption

  • Business will continue to operate indefinitely.
  • Enables long-term planning like taking loans or buying assets.
  • No intention to liquidate or shut down.

ğŸ”¸ 2.2 Accrual Assumption

Transactions are recorded when they occur, not when cash is exchanged.

Example:

  • Credit sales are recorded even if cash is not yet received.
  • Salaries due are recorded as expenses even if unpaid.

ğŸ”¸ 2.3 Consistency Principle

Use the same accounting methods year after year.

Allows meaningful comparison of financial data over time.

Example: Using the same depreciation method for assets.

ğŸ”¸ 2.4 Business Entity Concept

Business is separate from its owner.

All transactions are recorded only for the business, not the personal activities of the owner.

Example: Owner's withdrawals are recorded as drawings, not expenses.

ğŸ”¸ 2.5 Money Measurement Concept

Only transactions measurable in money are recorded.

Non-quantifiable factors like employee skill or reputation are not recorded.

Example:

  • Record "Machinery purchased for â‚¹5 lakh"
  • Ignore: "Owner's experience = 10 years"

ğŸ”¸ 2.6 Accounting Period Concept

Business life is divided into fixed time periods for reporting.

ğŸ”¸ 2.7 Full Disclosure Principle

All relevant and significant financial information must be disclosed.

Ensures transparency, trust, and better decision-making.

Includes footnotes, contingencies, etc.

ğŸ”¸ 2.8 Materiality Principle

Record only information that is important enough to influence decisions.

Minor errors that don't impact decisions can be ignored.

ğŸ”¸ 2.9 Prudence (Conservatism) Principle

  • Do not overstate assets or income.
  • Record possible losses but not unrealized gains.
  • Be cautious to avoid misleading financial position.

ğŸ”¸ 2.10 Dual Aspect Concept

  • Every transaction affects at least two accounts.
  • Basis of double-entry system.
  • Total debits = total credits
